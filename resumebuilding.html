Resume Tips

Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application by including keywords from the job description.

Quantify Achievements

Use numbers to demonstrate your impact (e.g., "Increased sales by 25%").

Keep It Concise

Limit your resume to 1-2 pages. Be selective about what you include.

Proofread Carefully

Typos and grammatical errors can eliminate you from consideration. Always proofread.

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "Managed," "Created," or "Implemented."

Choose the Right Format

Use reverse chronological format (most recent first) unless you have specific circumstances.

ATS Optimization

Make Your Resume ATS-Friendly

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes. Follow these tips to ensure your resume gets seen by human eyes:

  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Work Experience," "Education")
  • Include keywords from the job description
  • Use a clean, simple format without tables or columns
  • Save your resume as a Word document or PDF (unless specified otherwise)
  • Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman
  • Avoid headers and footers which can confuse ATS
